Demo & Usage

To see an ASCII Art Welcome Mat in action, just perform the following complicated steps anywhere on this site:

  1. Right click
  2. View page source

To use a Welcome Mat on your own site:

  1. Click the Welcome Mat you'd like to use, and copy it.
  2. Paste it somewhere near the beginning of your HTML document. All Welcome Mats should validate.
  3. ...and Voila!

Submit

To submit your own Welcome Mat to be added to this site, just tweet me a link to a plain text file or some other place where your ASCII Welcome Mat lives.

Rules & Stuff:

  1. Your Welcome Mat should be no more than 80 characters wide.
  2. It should be welcoming, funny, or awesome. Some combination of those three things would be even better.
  3. Tell me your name and where you want your name link to.
  4. This is for funsies and for sharing, so your Welcome Mat will be displayed on this site with a Creative Commons Attribution License (meaning you must be attributed, but others are free to share or remix your work). If you have an issue with this, tweet me and we can discuss.

About

This site was created because I think web designers and developers these days are far too concerned with serious things like "performance" and "search engine optimization." I miss the Good Old Days™ when <marquee> and <blink> tags where the norm, and we used <table>s for everything under the sun.

The goal was to get some experience creating a responsive prototype using Jekyll, SASS/Compass, ZURB Foundation 4, and hosting on Amazon S3. Much learning was had.

Everything on this site is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported License, so please remix, share, then send me your creations so we can keep ASCII Art and Good Manners alive.
